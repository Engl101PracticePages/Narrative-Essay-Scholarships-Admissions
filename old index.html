Narrative Essay: Exploring Academic Opportunities

Research an academic opportunity that connects to your goals, such as a transfer program, summer program, or scholarship. Then identify that opportunity’s own essay prompt or written response questions and explain how participating in it would support your academic or professional future.

Important
Keep this worksheet open while you work. Open your scholarship or program research in separate tabs. Do not count on reopening this page later and finding everything still here. When you finish, use Print / Save as PDF and upload that PDF for credit.

1. Choose an opportunity type

Choose something you are generally eligible for based on your major, academic goals, transfer plans, background, or student status.

You should choose an opportunity that is a real fit for you. Do not choose something that clearly does not match your situation. For summer programs, it is okay if the specific program is not open right now as long as it is something you would realistically be able to apply to next year.

4. What does “prompt” mean here?

This is one of the most important parts of the assignment.

A prompt is the writing question or task that the scholarship, transfer program, or summer program asks applicants to respond to.

For this assignment, you are not listing my class directions as the prompt.

You are looking for the opportunity’s own essay question or written response questions, such as:

  • Describe a challenge you have overcome.
  • Explain why you are a strong candidate for this opportunity.
  • Discuss your academic goals and how this program will support them.

If the opportunity does not require applicants to write an essay or answer written response questions, you cannot use it for this assignment.

This opportunity will not work for this assignment. You need to choose a scholarship, transfer application, or program that includes an essay prompt or separate written response questions.
